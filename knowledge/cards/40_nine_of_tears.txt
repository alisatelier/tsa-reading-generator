CARD ID: 40
CANONICAL TSA NAME (always use this name in the reading): Nine of Tears
RWS EQUIVALENT (reference only; do not replace TSA name): Nine of Cups
SUIT: Tears
PIP: Nine

NAMING RULE:
- In all writing outputs, call this card "Nine of Tears".

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (UPRIGHT):
1) Start with Tears: emotional fulfillment, connection, intuitive resonance.
2) Add Nine (Integration): achievements have unlocked.
3) Translate: emotional satisfaction, contentment, and self-trust—your heart knows what it wants and can receive it.
4) Practical step: celebrate what is working; practice receiving without guilt or over-explaining.

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (REVERSED):
1) Keep Tears domain.
2) Use Nine (Reversed): setback; the strides you’ve gained are lost.
3) Name the friction: feeling emotionally “backslide,” craving validation, or mistaking a bad day for a bad life.
4) Correction: return to your emotional basics—rest, boundaries, honest needs, supportive connection.

QUICK SIGNALS:
- Supportive: contentment; emotional reward; receiving; heart confidence.
- Challenging: emptiness after achievement; emotional regression; needing external approval.

JOURNAL PROMPT:
- Where am I allowed to receive more—without earning it harder or shrinking it down?
