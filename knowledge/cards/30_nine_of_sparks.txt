CARD ID: 30
CANONICAL TSA NAME (always use this name in the reading): Nine of Sparks
RWS EQUIVALENT (reference only; do not replace TSA name): Nine of Wands
SUIT: Sparks
PIP: Nine

NAMING RULE:
- In all writing outputs, call this card "Nine of Sparks".

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (UPRIGHT):
1) Start with Sparks: soul-work, creativity, ambition.
2) Add Nine (Integration): achievements have unlocked.
3) Translate: you’ve gained competence; your work is maturing; confidence comes from lived proof.
4) Practical step: consolidate—document your process, refine your offer, and build a sustainable rhythm.

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (REVERSED):
1) Keep Sparks domain.
2) Use Nine (Reversed): setback; the strides you’ve gained are lost.
3) Name the friction: discouragement, fatigue, losing momentum after progress, or comparing yourself backwards.
4) Correction: return to foundations—one small daily practice, one weekly checkpoint, and one supportive resource.

QUICK SIGNALS:
- Supportive: earned confidence; mastery emerging; integration of lessons.
- Challenging: fatigue; discouragement; feeling like you’re “back at square one” (even if you’re not).

JOURNAL PROMPT:
- What proof already exists that I have grown—even if I feel tired today?
