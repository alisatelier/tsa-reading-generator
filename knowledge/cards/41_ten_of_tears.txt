CARD ID: 41
CANONICAL TSA NAME (always use this name in the reading): Ten of Tears
RWS EQUIVALENT (reference only; do not replace TSA name): Ten of Cups
SUIT: Tears
PIP: Ten

NAMING RULE:
- In all writing outputs, call this card "Ten of Tears".

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (UPRIGHT):
1) Start with Tears: heart bonds, emotional truth, connection.
2) Add Ten (Completion): you have arrived.
3) Translate: emotional fulfillment, harmony, and a stable connection—family, chosen family, community, or inner peace.
4) Practical step: anchor the feeling with a ritual—gratitude, commitment, quality time, shared vision, or repair.

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (REVERSED):
1) Keep Tears domain.
2) Use Ten (Reversed): completion, but not in the way you hoped.
3) Name the friction: a “happy ending” expectation doesn’t match reality; disillusionment; misalignment in emotional needs.
4) Correction: redefine what fulfillment means; choose truth over performance; make one honest request or boundary.

QUICK SIGNALS:
- Supportive: emotional harmony; belonging; heart completion; stable love.
- Challenging: misaligned expectations; family tension; chasing a picture-perfect outcome.

JOURNAL PROMPT:
- What does true emotional fulfillment mean for me, beyond appearances or expectations?
