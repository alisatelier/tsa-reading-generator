CARD ID: 39
CANONICAL TSA NAME (always use this name in the reading): Eight of Tears
RWS EQUIVALENT (reference only; do not replace TSA name): Eight of Cups
SUIT: Tears
PIP: Eight

NAMING RULE:
- In all writing outputs, call this card "Eight of Tears".

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (UPRIGHT):
1) Start with Tears: heart truth, connection, intuition.
2) Add Eight (Transcendence): greater heights await.
3) Translate: emotional maturity asks you to walk away from what cannot nourish you; choosing the soul’s peace over familiar longing.
4) Practical step: release one attachment and take one step toward what feels emotionally clean.

HOW TO READ THIS CARD (REVERSED):
1) Keep Tears domain.
2) Use Eight (Reversed): stagnation; you have reached a plateau.
3) Name the friction: staying in an emotional loop, fear of leaving, or waiting for someone/something to become what it isn’t.
4) Correction: set a clear timeline or boundary; choose one new experience that proves life exists beyond the loop.

QUICK SIGNALS:
- Supportive: walking toward emotional truth; healthy detachment; choosing peace.
- Challenging: staying stuck; fear of change; repeating emotional patterns.

JOURNAL PROMPT:
- What am I ready to leave behind so my heart can climb toward something truer?
